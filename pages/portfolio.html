Mobile hydrology is a web application where hydrologists, scientists, students and citizens can create their own history of water in their backyard, community, stream, spring or lake and join a global community of hydrology scientists and engineers who study and manage our water resources. Mobile Hydrology uses DBSCAN image processing to read water gauges planted in the reservoir and adds the information collected from the water gauges to a database.


Responsibilites and Technologies


  • Worked as a Front-End Software Engineer
  • Designed the logo using Canva, Photoshop and Illustrator
  • Designed the UI
  • Used HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap and JavaScript for Front-End Work
  • Used Grails and IntelliJ for Development
  • Used Taiga for Agile/Scrum Project Managment
  • Used gitHub for collaboration
  • Used on campus Linux machines and personal Macbook for development

ACL Risk Estimator is a web application to provide means for Upper Peninsula (UP) high school (HS) coaches to get ACL injury risk assessment for their athletes and some education on prevention and mitigation of ACL injuries. Coaches or Athlete Trainers can upload videos of their athletes performing a jump, which later can be assessed by an injury expert to rate the ACL at risk on a scale of 1-10 along with feedback.

Responsibilites and Technologies

  • Acted as a Scrum Master and utilized Scrum and Kanban to manage the project
  • Used Taiga for Kanban, user-stories, issue tracking and task management
